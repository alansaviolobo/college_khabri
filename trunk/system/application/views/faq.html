Frequently Asked Questions


1) How can I figure out which is the right college and branch for me?


We have provided a framework to help you figure out right choice of colleges and stream for you.


2) How do I search colleges?


Once you have decided the branch, go to search page. Select the branch name. Specify other parameters important to you e.g. university, district, minority status, fees etc. The search results page will show the colleges and branches that match your search criteria.


3) What is projected MHT CET/AIEEE score?


Projected MHT CET/AIEEE score is your estimate of how much you would score in MHT CET/AIEEE. It need not be perfect; you can enter a rough estimate. You can change the projected score any number of times.


4) Why is projected MHT CET / AIEEE score mandatory?


CollegeKhabri.com helps you decide the right colleges for you. We show the list of the colleges where you stand a chance of getting the admission based on last year’s cut off. So it is important that you enter your projected MHT CET score on your profile page. Once the Merit Rank for MHT CET and AIEEE is out, you will be required to enter your merit rank on your profile. Your merit rank will give you more accurate search results.


5) What is popularity rank?


Popularity rank is the rank of the college or branch based on the merit rank of the students admitted to that college or branch. The more popular a college, the lower its merit rank.


6) What is the home university?


Home university depends on the district from which you appeared for the HSC exam.


7) Why do I need to enter the home university?


70% of the seats are reserved for candidates from home university and 30% are reserved for candidates from out of home university. If you enter your home university in your profile, we will show you appropriate cut off applicable to you as well as your chances of getting into a branch.


8) What is choice code?


Choice code is a unique number given to a branch in a college.


9) What does “My Chances” mean on search results page?


“My Chances” column indicates your chances of getting into a branch in a college. The chances are categorized as high, medium and low. Low chances means it’s tough for a candidate to get an admission into that college through Centralized Admission Procedure. My chances do not take into consideration Institute Level seats. If you still have any doubts, send us your queries. We will get back to you as soon as possible.
